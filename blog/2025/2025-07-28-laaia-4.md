## Null Spaces, Column Spaces and Linear Transformations

> Null Space (based on number of columns, since $\textbf{x}$ is based on $A$'s number of columns) and Column Space (based on number of rows since, it is the linear combination of all columns resulting in number of rows) are in different "universes"

## Coordinate Systems

> An **IMPORTANT** reason for specifying the *basis* for a vector space $V$ is to **impose a coordinate system** on $V$. If the basis contains $n$ vectors, then the coordinate system will make $V$ act like $\R^n$.

> The Coordinate System we always use is the **STANDARD BASIS** such that $[\textbf{x}]_\epsilon=\textbf{x}$

$$
\textbf{x}=(1, 6) = 1\cdot\begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix} + 6\cdot\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}
$$

$P_\mathcal{B}=[\textbf{b}_1 \ldots \textbf{b}_n]$ (**change-of-coordinates matrix**) 

Vector equation $\textbf{x}=c_1\textbf{b}_1+\ldots+c_n\textbf{b}_n$ is equivalent to
$$
\textbf{x} = P_\mathcal{B} [\textbf{x}]_\mathcal{B}
$$

since the basis vectors as a matrix transforms the coordinate vector into the standard coordinates.
